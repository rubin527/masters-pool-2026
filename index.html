⛳ The Draft

1.
Before the tournament, the Commissioner conducts a snake draft in which each of the 13 owners selects 6 golfers from the full Masters field.
2.
Once a golfer is selected, no other owner may claim them. The field depletes as the draft progresses.
3.
The draft order reverses each round. The owner who picks first in Round 1 picks last in Round 2, and so on. This is the snake.
4.
Each pick has a 90-second clock. If the clock expires, the system automatically selects the highest-ranked available golfer by odds.

How the snake draft works — 13 owners, 6 rounds

Gold squares mark where the order turns. Odd rounds go left to right, even rounds right to left.

📊 Scoring

1.
Your team score is the combined score-to-par of your best 4 golfers after all four rounds. Lowest score wins.
2.
Your remaining 2 golfers sit on the bench and do not affect your score — unless a cut penalty forces them into your top 4.
3.
Missed cut: A golfer who misses the cut is assigned the same score as the last-place finisher among those who made the cut. This score updates live as Saturday and Sunday rounds are played. Missing the cut is intended to hurt.
4.
A golfer who withdraws (WD) after making the cut receives the same last-place penalty as a missed cut.

🏆 Winning

1.
The owner with the lowest combined team score after the conclusion of the Masters on Sunday wins the pool.
2.
The leaderboard updates live throughout all four rounds. Check it often.
